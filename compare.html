Pros & Cons · Deep Dive
Jenzabar ONE vs Workday Student

The decision most institutions are weighing in 2026 — the proven SQL-friendly workhorse vs the modern cloud platform.

Jenzabar ONE

✓ Pros
▶Full T-SQL access — your team can write any query directly
▶Built-in IPEDS surveys — no third-party tool needed
▶Fastest implementation in the category — 12–18 months realistic
▶Strong NCAA and financial aid modules out of the box
▶Active user community (JENZABAR USERS GROUP) with shared scripts
✗ Cons
▶UI feels dated compared to cloud-native competitors
▶Smaller talent pool than Banner — harder to hire experienced staff
▶On-premise installs require significant IT infrastructure
▶Mobile experience lags behind modern SaaS platforms

Workday Student

✓ Pros
▶Modern cloud-native architecture — no servers to maintain
▶Seamless integration if institution already uses Workday HCM/Finance
▶Beautiful, consistent UI across all modules
▶Automatic updates — always on latest version
✗ Cons
▶No direct SQL access — reporting locked to Prism and Worksheets
▶IPEDS and NCAA reporting require custom builds or third-party tools
▶Implementation 2–4 years — significant institutional disruption
▶Highest total cost of ownership in the category
🏆
// VERDICT
For most institutions under 15,000 students with an active SQL reporting team, Jenzabar ONE delivers better ROI and faster time-to-value. Workday Student is the right choice only if your institution is already deeply invested in the Workday ecosystem and has the budget and timeline for a 3+ year implementation. Don't let the modern UI be the deciding factor — reporting flexibility will matter every single week.
